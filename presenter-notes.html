STEADYWRK Trainer Script - Plain Version

Private screen. Read naturally. Share only the presentation deck.

Do Not Forget These Points

1. What dispatch meansReceive a job, understand it, find the right technician, keep everyone updated, collect proof.
2. Basic wordsWork order, technician, NTE, scope, ETA, proof, update, sourcing.
3. What we teachCalls, English, sourcing, updates, AI tools, digital tools, proof, professional behavior.
4. CertificateSCOA proves they trained on these skills. It is connected to IGNITE FOUNDATION.
5. Safety ruleIf unsure, ask. Do not guess. Do not invent information. Do not hide problems.
6. PracticeThey must repeat the call script out loud. This is the most important activity.

Opening

"Welcome. Today we are doing dispatcher training. A dispatcher helps move a job from problem to solution. You receive the job, understand what is needed, find the right technician, give clear updates, and make sure there is proof when the job is done."
"This training is also part of what we teach in IGNITE FOUNDATION. The certificate connected to this path is called SCOA: STEADYWRK Certified Operations Associate."
Keep it simple: Do not talk too long here. Start the slides quickly.

Slide 1 - What This Training Is

"By the end, I want you to understand the full dispatcher flow: read the work order, call technicians, confirm details, update the team, use AI tools carefully, and close the job with proof."
Ask: "Has anyone here ever had to coordinate people by phone or WhatsApp?" Connect their answer to dispatch.

Slide 2 - The Business Model

"A work order is a job request. It usually has a budget. The budget is called NTE. NTE means 'not to exceed.' If the NTE is 450 dollars, we should not go above 450 unless someone approves it."
"Our job is to find a technician who can do the work correctly, on time, and inside the budget."
Example: "If the budget is $450 and the technician charges $300, there is room. If the technician asks for $600, we need approval or another option."

Slide 3 - The Dispatcher Flow

"The flow is: alert, read, source, call, confirm, update, proof, close. Let's say it once together: alert, read, source, call, confirm, update, proof, close."
Explain: Source means find possible technicians. Proof means photos, notes, time, invoice, or anything the client needs to confirm the work.

Slide 4 - Reading The Work Order

"Before calling anyone, read the job. Look for six things: location, trade, scope, urgency, NTE, and proof required."
Location = where. Trade = type of work. Scope = what problem. Urgency = how soon. NTE = budget. Proof = what we need after.
Do on screen: Point to the sample job and make them answer each item.

Slide 5 - Opening The Call

"The call opening should be short. Do not give too much information at the start. First, check if they can help."
"Hey, good morning, I'm [Name]. Looking for a plumber in Utica for a small commercial job today."
Practice: You read it once. They repeat it. Correct only the important parts: city, trade, today.

Slide 6 - Checking If They Fit

"After the opening, ask simple questions. Can they do this trade? Can they go today? What time can they arrive? Can they send photos? Are they independent or owner-operated?"
"Quick one: commercial restroom sink clog. Can you take a look today?"
"What time can you arrive? And you can send before and after photos, correct?"
Ask: "Why do we ask about photos?" Answer: because photos help close the job and prove the work.

Slide 7 - Confirming The Technician

"If the technician can help, confirm the next step. Send the address and job notes. Ask them to reply with ETA when they are on the way. ETA means estimated time of arrival."
"Perfect. I'll text the address and job notes now. Reply with ETA when you are on the way."
Important: If the job changes, the technician should stop and tell us first. No surprise extra work.

Slide 8 - Updates

"An update should be clear enough that another person can continue the job without calling you. Do not write only 'called tech.' Write what happened and what is next."
Good update: "JOB-7018: called 4 plumbers. One available at 2:30. Waiting for photo confirmation. Next call in 10 minutes."
Practice: Ask them to create one update using: JOB - action - status - next step.

Slide 9 - Scheduling

"If we have a main technician and a backup, we must keep them spaced apart. If two technicians arrive at the same time, it creates confusion and possible extra cost."
Ask: "What can go wrong if two techs arrive together?" Let them answer: confusion, double charge, bad experience.

Slide 10 - IGNITE FOUNDATION And SCOA

"IGNITE FOUNDATION is the full program. It is six weeks, two sessions each week, in Aqaba and Amman. Each cohort has twenty seats. The program teaches operations English, dispatch basics, technician calls, sourcing, digital tools, AI tools, professional behavior, and a final practice simulation."
"Graduates receive SCOA: STEADYWRK Certified Operations Associate. The certificate is serialized and can be checked at steadywrk.app/verify."
Arabic if useful: "أساسيات الحقبة الذكية تدريب عملي لتعلّم تشغيل غرفة العمليات واستخدام الأدوات الحديثة بطريقة صحيحة."

Slide 11 - AI Tools

"We also teach AI tools. AI can help summarize a work order, translate a message, make a checklist, or draft an update. But AI can be wrong. Before using the answer, check the real work order."
Simple rule: "Use AI for help. Check before using."
Example: "If AI says the job is not urgent, but the ticket says water is backing up, trust the ticket."

Slide 12 - What They Learn

"The skills are practical: English for work orders and calls, how to read tickets, how to call technicians, how to find local contractors, how to use WhatsApp and scheduling tools, how to use AI safely, and how to close a job with proof."
Ask: "Which skill do you think you need most: English, calls, sourcing, AI tools, or updates?"

Slide 13 - Call Script Practice

"Now we practice the technician call. I will read one line. Then you repeat it. Do not worry about a perfect accent. The goal is clear meaning."
1. "Hey, good morning, I'm [Name]. Looking for a plumber in Utica for a small commercial job today."
2. "Quick one: commercial restroom sink clog. Can you take a look today?"
3. "What time can you arrive? And you can send before and after photos, correct?"
4. "Perfect. I'll text the address and job notes now. Reply with ETA when you are on the way."
After practice: Ask: "What information did we confirm?" Answer: trade, availability, ETA, photos, phone number.

Close

"That is the basic job. Read clearly. Call clearly. Update clearly. Use tools carefully. Close with proof. That is what we train in IGNITE FOUNDATION, and that is what SCOA is meant to show."
Final reminder: If someone asks something you do not know, say: "Good question. I will confirm and come back to you." Do not guess.
