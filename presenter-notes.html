STEADYWRK Trainer Script

Keep this on your private screen. Share the animated deck only.

Opening

"Welcome. This is STEADYWRK dispatch training. Today you are learning how a real operations desk works: read the job, find the right technician, make the call, update the team, and close with proof. This also connects to our IGNITE FOUNDATION program and the SCOA certificate."
Do not over-explain. Point at slide 1 and say: "This is the path: dispatch skill plus certificate."

Slide 2 - The Model

"The business is simple. A work order comes in with a budget. We find a local technician who can do the work inside that budget. The difference is the margin. So your job affects real money."
Ask: "Where can money be lost?" Answer: wrong tech, bad price, late ETA, weak notes, missing proof.

Slide 3 - Your Loop

"This is the daily loop. Alert comes in. You read it. You source. You call. You secure the tech. You update the team. You collect photos and close. The important part is this: we do not always hand you ready leads. Finding local techs is part of the job."
Tie to cert: "Local sourcing is one of the skills behind SCOA."

Slide 4 - Work Order

"Before you call anyone, read the job. Look for location, trade, budget, problem, urgency, and proof required. If you skip this step, every call after it gets weaker."
Ask them to name the location, trade, NTE, and proof from the sample ticket.

Slide 5 - Opening Call

"Keep the opening normal and clear. Say the city, the trade, and that it is a small commercial job today. Do not sound like a script. Do not start with a long explanation."
Use the line on the slide. If they struggle with English, slow it down and have them repeat it once.

Slide 6 - Qualification

"Now check if they can actually help. What is the job? Can they go today? What time? Can they provide photos? Do they understand it is commercial? This is not a friendly chat. It is a quick fit check."
Ask: "What do we need before we send the address?" Answer: availability, fit, phone number, basic agreement.

Slide 7 - Locking

"Once the technician is interested, then you explain the simple rules. Photos are required. Updates are required. If something changes, they tell us before doing extra work. The order matters: first interest, then rules."
Keep this practical. No big speech.

Slide 8 - 30-Minute Rule

"Silence causes problems. Every update should say what happened, what is next, and who owns the next step. If another dispatcher takes over, they should not have to guess."
Ask them for one clean update sentence.

Slide 9 - Scheduling

"Do not create two problems while trying to solve one. If you book a backup, leave enough spacing. If two techs arrive together, that is confusion, not backup."
Point to the two-hour rule.

Slide 10 - Certificate Path

"This is where the training connects to the program. IGNITE FOUNDATION is six weeks, two sessions per week, in Aqaba and Amman, with twenty seats per cohort. It teaches dispatch, operations English, AI tool use, sourcing, documentation, and professional conduct. Graduates receive SCOA: STEADYWRK Certified Operations Associate, verifiable at steadywrk.app/verify."
Arabic line if needed: "أساسيات الحقبة الذكية هي بوابتكم لتعلّم تشغيل غرفة العمليات في عصر الذكاء الاصطناعي."

Slide 11 - AI Copilot

"AI is part of the work, but it is not the boss. We can use it to summarize a ticket, make a checklist, draft a clean update, or help with English. But we must check it. Always check the real work order: address, scope, NTE, SLA, client rules, technician availability, and proof needed."
Say clearly: "The rule is prompt, verify, decide. Not prompt, copy, send."

Slide 12 - What You Get

"Here is what the program gives you. You learn operations English, dispatch basics, how to read tickets, how to call technicians, how to use AI carefully, how to update the team, and how to close jobs with proof. You also learn the local market: Aqaba, Amman, hotels, facility maintenance, and construction. At the end, the capstone simulation tests the real behavior, and SCOA gives proof that you completed the standard."
Keep it short. This is the offer. Do not sound like an ad. Sound like: "Here are the skills you leave with."

Slide 13 - Read The Call Script Together

"Now we are going to read the technician call together. I will read one line first, then you repeat it. The goal is not perfect accent. The goal is clear, calm, useful English."
Read line 1 from the slide. Pause. Make them repeat. Then line 2, line 3, line 4. Correct only clarity: city, trade, today, ETA, photos.
"Line one: Hey, good morning, I'm [Name]. Looking for a plumber in Utica for a small commercial job today."
"Line two: Quick one: commercial restroom sink clog. Can you take a look today?"
"Line three: What time can you arrive? And you can send before and after photos, correct?"
"Line four: Perfect. I'll text the address and job notes now. Reply with ETA when you are on the way."

Close

"The goal is not to memorize slides. The goal is to act like an operations person: read clearly, speak clearly, use AI carefully, update clearly, source locally, and close with proof. That is what the certificate is meant to show."
Then start the live exercise or Q&A.
