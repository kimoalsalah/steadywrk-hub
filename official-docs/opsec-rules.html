Dept DISPATCH OPS

§ Information Security
01
Queue Privacy
⚠ Disciplinary Action
  • You see and discuss your assigned queue ONLY
  • NEVER ask about or discuss other dispatchers' jobs, queues, or performance
  • Your channel #d-you is private for a reason — treat it that way
  • Queue information is compartmentalized by design; cross-queue visibility is a security breach
02
Client Shield Protocol
🔴 Grounds for Dismissal
  • NEVER mention our client names to technicians
  • NEVER say: "Dollar General", "Walgreens", "UPS", or any other client name
  • To the tech, it's always "a facility we coordinate" — nothing more
  • This protects our contracts and prevents techs from going direct to clients
IF A TECH ASKS "Who is this for?"
→ "We are STEADYWRK Facilities Network. We coordinate maintenance for this site."
03
Client Call Firewall
🔴 Grounds for Dismissal
  • If a store manager, corporate representative, or client contact calls you:
1. MUTE or HANG UP immediately
2. Escalate to the Team Leader (Kevin)
3. Do NOT answer their questions
4. Do NOT redirect them
5. Do NOT attempt to be helpful
  • You are STRICTLY tech-facing — client communication is above your clearance
  • Speaking directly to a client representative is grounds for immediate dismissal
  • Simply hang up and escalate — there is no exception to this rule
§ Financial Security
04
Payment Terms Protocol
🔴 Grounds for Dismissal
  • NEVER discuss payment terms on the initial phone call
  • NEVER discuss specific payment methods (PayPal, Zelle, Cash App, Venmo) on the phone
  • Let the Tech Brief handle payment terms in writing
  • Payment is 5–7 business days after completion + photos + summary
IF A TECH ASKS ABOUT PAYMENT:
→ "We pay standard ACH or check through our accounting platform. I'll text you the link."
05
NTE Discipline
⚠ Disciplinary Action
  • NTE is a STRICT CEILING, not a target
  • NEVER disclose the NTE amount to the technician
  • Aim for 30–50% below NTE to preserve margin
  • Approve quotes under NTE within 10 minutes when tech is on-site
  • Do NOT haggle on-site over minor amounts ($10–$50) — speed closes deals
§ Operational Discipline
06
Real-Time Coaching Mic Discipline
⚠ Disciplinary Action
  • Do NOT mute your microphone in training huddles while on shift
  • The Team Leader must hear your live calls from A to Z
  • This enables real-time coaching and error correction
  • Muting during calls = missed coaching opportunities = performance risk
07
Recording Protocol
⚠ Disciplinary Action
  • Record 100% of outbound calls — no exceptions
  • Press record BEFORE you start speaking
  • If you forget to record a call, flag it to the Team Leader immediately
  • Unrecorded calls that aren't flagged = disciplinary action
  • Recordings are used for coaching, quality scoring, and dispute resolution
08
Address Release Protocol
🔴 Unauthorized Dispatch
  • NEVER send the job address to a tech until the Team Leader approves
  • Sending an address before approval = unauthorized dispatch
WORKFLOW: Source → Vet → Get Team Leader Approval → THEN Send Tech Brief with Address
  • No shortcuts. No exceptions. The chain of approval exists for operational security.
§ Service Recovery & Accountability
09
Missed Scope Protocol
◈ Mandatory Process
  • If a tech misses part of the scope, own the mistake
  • Never blame the tech for scope you didn't communicate clearly
  • Offer a flat $50 return fee to have them come back, complete it, and get the sign-off sheet
SCRIPT: "Hey, I'm sorry, my bad, I missed a small part…"
→ Offer $50 return trip fee → Complete scope → Get sign-off
§ Communication & Reporting
10
Communication Standards
⚠ Disciplinary Action
  • Set a 30-minute timer on your phone
  • Update #reporting-daily every 30 minutes with: [JOB-ID] - [ACTION] - [NEXT STEP]
  • Reply to tech callbacks within 4 hours
  • Never leave a warm lead cold — momentum is everything
  • Submit Clockify and ActivityWatch screenshots daily
11
Scheduling Rules
⚠ Disciplinary Action
  • Main and backup ETAs must be spaced at least 2 HOURS apart
  • Never double-book techs at the same time
  • If main tech completes, call backup to cancel politely
  • If techs see each other on-site, we lose both — this is a critical failure
§ Documentation & Compliance
12
Photo & Documentation Standards
◈ Mandatory Process
  • 6 BEFORE photos and 6 AFTER photos — minimum, strict standard
  • Store manager must sign the Sign-Off Sheet with Job ID
  • Tech must send work summary after completion
  • Tech must submit W-9 for payment setup
  • No photos = no payment processing — there are zero exceptions
REQUIRED DELIVERABLES:
☐ 6× Before Photos  |  ☐ 6× After Photos  |  ☐ Signed Sign-Off Sheet
☐ Work Summary  |  ☐ W-9 on File
