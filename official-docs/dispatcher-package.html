The Model — How We Make Money

STEADYWRK wins facilities work orders for major commercial properties nationwide and subcontracts them to local, independent, owner-operator technicians. You own your queue end to end.

Revenue Pipeline
GET WO → Call Local Techs → Vet → Secure One → Coordinate Visit → Photos + Completion → Invoice → $ PAID

The spread between what the job pays and what the tech costs is our margin. The faster you secure a good tech, the faster we earn.

02

Your Daily Loop

  1. A work order drops in your Slack channel #d-you — already formatted.
  2. Build your call list immediately using Yelp, Google, and BBB. Sourcing is 90% of the job. You do NOT get ready leads.
  3. Call the techs you source. Vet. Secure one. Lock a time + price.
  4. Send the Tech Brief (see Section 7) once approved. Coordinate.
  5. Drive it to completion → photos → invoice → paid.
  6. Report status in your channel so the board tracks you.
03

Reading a Work Order

Standard WO Format
Work Order Template
📋 NEW WORK ORDER [ | 🚨 SUPER EMERGENCY ] JOB-260612-7018 <store / site> — <street> <city>, <state> Trade: <trade> Scope: <what to do> NTE: $<price cap> Urgency: <date> Notes: before + after photos required · Sign-Off Sheet · 30-min ETA call · no on-site payment 📞 Call list: [None / Sourcing Required]
  • NTE = "Not To Exceed" — the most we can spend. Your tech's price must come in under NTE.
  • Urgency = when it must be done. Overdue/today = drop everything.
  • Call list = Empty by default. Sourcing is 100% your responsibility. If a call list is ever attached, it is strictly a historical backup list.
04

The Call — Your Script

Memorize the rhythm, not the exact words.

① The Local Opening

"Hey, good morning/afternoon, I'm [Name]. Looking for a [trade] in [city]?"

  • Do NOT say "By the way" or "Hello, can you hear me?"
  • Do NOT say the state right away. Sound local.
  • If asked how you found them: "Yeah, I recently moved into the neighborhood and am looking around..."
② The Quick Pitch

"Cool. Quick one — [scope of work, 5–7 words]. Can you help?"

③ Fast Qualification

"When can you go — anything today?"
"Free estimate on site, right?"
If yes: "Perfect, sending the address. Good number?"

④ Locking & Rules

"One last thing — you work independent, correct?"
"Cool, I'll text a couple house rules — quick read before you head out, sites are picky. Thanks man, text me anytime."

⛔ CRITICAL WARNINGS — OPSEC & PRICING
  1. NEVER name the client. Say "We are STEADYWRK Facilities Network. We coordinate maintenance for this site."
  2. NO payment talk on the phone. Let the texted brief handle it.
  3. NO on-site haggling. Approve quotes under NTE within 10 minutes.
  4. Do not send the address until the Team Leader approves the tech.
05

Vetting Standards — Who's a YES

✅ Green Light — Proceed
  • Owner-operator or small independent
  • Available in the required window
  • Price comes in under NTE
  • Clean, professional presentation
  • Licensed if required by the trade
  • OK to present as our team on site
❌ Red Flag — Pass Immediately
  • Big franchise (Roto-Rooter, Mr. Rooter, etc.)
  • Can't make the time window
  • Pricing exceeds NTE
  • Won't do before/after photos
  • Evasive or unprofessional on the phone

The Tech Brief — What You SEND the Technician

⚠ Only send AFTER Team Leader approval

Copy, fill the brackets, send by text message. Never send this before the tech is approved.

Where Techs Come From

  1. Yelp Search by trade, filter 4.0★+, select "Open Now."
  2. Google Maps Local techs near the job site, call for same-day availability.
  3. BBB Accredited + owner-operated businesses. Reliable vetting baseline.
  4. Craigslist Skilled Trades section — independent operators posting services.
  5. Facebook Marketplace + local trade groups. Community-sourced leads.
⚡ Rule of Thumb

Always favor owner-operators. Never call the big franchises — they won't work under our model and they'll waste your time.

OPSEC & Operations Standards — Non-Negotiable

Standard Rule
Queue Privacy You see your assigned queue only. Never access or discuss other dispatchers' queues.
Client Shield Never mention client names to techs. Ever.
Client Call Firewall If a client/manager calls you, mute/hang up immediately and escalate to Team Leader. You are strictly tech-facing; speaking to the client is grounds for dismissal.
Mic Discipline Do not mute your microphone in training huddles while on shift. Real-time coaching requires an open mic.
Missed Scope Own the mistake as "my bad / oversight" and negotiate a $50 return fee.
🔒 These rules protect the entire operation. Violations are not coached — they're terminal.
