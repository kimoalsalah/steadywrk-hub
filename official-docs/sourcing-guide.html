Section 01

Why Sourcing Matters

Sourcing is the single most impactful skill in dispatch. Understanding why elevates you from average to top performer.

🎯

You Are the Lead Engine

You do NOT get ready leads. Sourcing is your primary responsibility. Every job starts with you finding the right technician.

⚡

Speed = Revenue

The faster you source quality techs, the faster jobs close, the more revenue we generate. Minutes matter.

📊

The Performance Gap

A strong sourcer closes 3+ jobs/day. A weak sourcer wastes hours on bad leads. The difference is technique, not luck.

💡
Bottom line: Every minute you spend sourcing inefficiently is money left on the table. Master these methods and you will consistently outperform. Your sourcing speed directly determines your daily close rate.
Section 02

The Sourcing Stack

Five platforms, ranked by priority. Master them in order — Yelp alone should cover 60%+ of your sourcing needs.

1
🔴 Yelp
Primary Source

How to Search

Go to yelp.com
Search: [trade] + [city] (e.g., "Plumber Clinton NY")
Filter: 4.0★+ rating, "Open Now"
Target: Owner-operators and small shops (1-5 employees)

What to Look For

✅ Real photos of completed work
✅ Detailed reviews mentioning the owner by name
✅ Business responds to reviews
🚩 National chains — skip immediately
🚩 No reviews or stock photos
💎 Pro Tip

Sort by "Highest Rated" first, then check availability. Quality beats quantity in sourcing.

🗺️ Google Maps
Secondary Source

How to Search

Search: "[trade] near [city]"
Look at Google Business profiles
Check: Reviews, photos, hours, website
Call ones with 4.0★+ and a real local address
💎 Pro Tip

Look at "People also search for" at the bottom of results for additional options you might have missed. Google often surfaces smaller operators there.

What to Check

✅ Real street address (not a P.O. Box)
✅ Recent reviews within the last 3 months
✅ Photos of actual work and team
✅ Listed hours show they're currently open

Section 03

What Makes a Good Tech

Use this vetting checklist every time you evaluate a potential technician. A tech must pass ALL green criteria and fail NONE of the red criteria.

✅

PASS — Green Lights

  • ✓ Owner-operator or small independent (1-5 people)
  • ✓ Available within the urgency window
  • ✓ Price under NTE (aim 30-50% below)
  • ✓ Clean, professional appearance
  • ✓ Licensed if trade requires it
  • ✓ Willing to present as part of our team on-site
  • ✓ Has their own tools and vehicle
  • ✓ Responsive to texts and calls
❌

FAIL — Red Flags

  • ✗ Big national franchise (Roto-Rooter, Mr. Handyman, etc.)
  • ✗ Can't make the urgency window
  • ✗ Price over NTE
  • ✗ Won't take before/after photos
  • ✗ Evasive about licensing or insurance
  • ✗ Refuses to present as our team
  • ✗ Demands same-day payment only
  • ✗ Unresponsive or unreliable communication
Section 04

Speed Tips

Efficiency separates top sourcers from everyone else. These battle-tested techniques will cut your sourcing time in half.

⚡

Sourcing Speed Protocol

1 Line up 3-5 numbers BEFORE you start dialing — never search between calls
2 Open Yelp, Google Maps, and BBB in separate tabs side by side
3 Copy phone numbers to a notepad first — don't hunt for numbers mid-call
4 Dial back-to-back — don't pause between calls, momentum matters
5 If voicemail: leave a 10-second message and move to the next number
6 Texts after voicemails double your callback rate
7 Keep a personal list of good techs you've used before, organized by city
Section 05

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Every new dispatcher makes these errors. Learning to avoid them will save you hours of wasted time every single day.

⚠️

Watch Out For These Traps

⏰
Spending too long on one source — if Yelp gives you nothing in 5 minutes, immediately switch to Google Maps. Don't get stuck.
🏢
Calling franchises — they're too expensive and won't follow our procedures. National chains are always a waste of time.
😴
Waiting for leads — YOU are the lead engine. Nobody is sending you techs. You have to go find them.
🔄
Not having a backup plan — always source a backup tech. Your primary will fall through 30% of the time.
🌙
Calling during off-hours — always check "Open Now" filters. Calling closed businesses wastes time and burns leads.
