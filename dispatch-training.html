THE CALL: OPENING

No "How's your day". No robot voice. Be local.

EXACT SCRIPT:

"Hey, good morning, I'm [Name]. Looking for a plumber in Utica for a small commercial job today."
  • Clean, direct tone.
  • Only say the city, never the state. (Locals don't say the state).
  • If asked how you found them: "Moved into the neighborhood, looking for help."

THE CALL: QUALIFICATION

Pitch scope in 5-7 words. Lock the window.

STEP 1

"Cool. Quick one—unclogging a commercial restroom sink. Can you help?"

STEP 2

"When can you go—anything today?"

STEP 3

"Free estimate on site, right? Perfect, sending the address. Good number?"

THE CALL: LOCKING

Establish boundaries. No payment terms or app talks on the phone.

NEVER DISCUSS PAYMENT TERMS OR APPS (PAYPAL/ZELLE/CASHAPP) ON THE PHONE.

"One last thing—you work independent, correct?"

"Cool, I'll text a couple house rules—quick read before you head out, sites are picky. Thanks man, text me anytime."

If they ask: *"We pay standard ACH or check through our platform. Setup link is in the brief."* Do not argue payment apps.

OPERATIONS: 30-MIN RULE

Communication is oxygen. Silence is failure.

  • Set a physical 30-minute timer on your phone.
  • Update #reporting-daily every 30 minutes.
  • Format: [JOB-ID] - [ACTION TAKEN] - [NEXT STEP]
  • Reply to all technician callbacks within 4 hours.
  • Huddle Mic Discipline: Keep mic UNMUTED at all times on shift so Kevin can coach live.

SCHEDULING: SPACING

Always secure a backup. Never let them overlap.

2 HOURS MINIMUM

Main and backup ETAs must be spaced by at least two hours to prevent double-booking collisions on-site.
